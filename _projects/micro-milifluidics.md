During my undergraduate studies at Iowa State University, I worked on a range of projects in microfluidics and milifluidics under the guidance of Prof. Jaime Juárez.

These projects explored microrheology, colloidal systems, and soft material interactions at small scales. Specific efforts included the development of portable imaging viscometry devices, flexible circuits incorporating ferrofluids, self-propelled PDMS floaters, and acoustic manipulation and assembly of particles.

This work provided a foundation in fluid mechanics, soft materials, and experimental system design, which later informed my research in soft robotics and haptic interfaces.

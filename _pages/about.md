I am a Postdoctoral Researcher and Mechanical Engineer at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, where I work in the Human Resilience Technology Group. My current research focuses on soft robotic systems for search-and-rescue and operation in unstructured environments, including vine-inspired continuum robots with integrated sensing and actuation.

I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Purdue University, where I developed pneumatically actuated haptic interfaces for human–machine interaction. My doctoral research combined soft robotic hardware, fluidic logic control, and human-subject studies to investigate how haptic signals can be designed, adapted, and deployed for effective information transfer.

Across projects, I design and build mechatronic systems that integrate soft actuation, embedded sensing, and control. My broader interests include soft robotics, haptics, human–robot interaction, and the development of deployable robotic systems for real-world applications.

This project focused on the development of flexible capacitive force sensors for integration into soft robotic gripping systems.

The goal was to enable real-time feedback for regulating adhesion and interaction forces between soft grippers and objects. A parallel-plate capacitor architecture was implemented using elastomeric materials (PDMS and Ecoflex) as the dielectric layer and copper film electrodes on compliant substrates.

Sensors were fabricated in multiple form factors (10×10 mm and 25×25 mm), with dielectric thicknesses ranging from 400 μm to 1.2 mm. The resulting devices achieved measurable capacitance changes in the 1–20 pF range under applied force.

To interpret sensor signals, multiple readout approaches were explored, including a 555-timer-based circuit and capacitance-to-digital converters. This work provided practical experience in soft sensor fabrication, signal acquisition, and integration with mechatronic systems.
